# Claim Calibration Example

This example is synthetic and contains no source-paper text.

## Input

```text
The proposed method is very excellent and significantly improves all baselines. This proves that our method is robust and suitable for many real applications.
```

## Polished Version

```text
The proposed method achieves lower objective values than the compared baselines under the tested settings. These results indicate that the proposed search mechanism can improve solution quality in the evaluated scenarios, although broader validation is still needed before drawing general conclusions about robustness across applications.
```

## Why This Is Better

- Replaces promotional adjectives with measurable claims.
- Avoids "proves" when the evidence is empirical.
- Names the scope condition: "under the tested settings".
- Keeps the implication useful without overgeneralizing.

## Pattern

```text
[Method] achieves [measured effect] than [comparison target] under [tested condition]. These results indicate/support/suggest [scoped implication], while [limitation or future validation] remains to be examined.
```
